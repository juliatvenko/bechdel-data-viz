The Bechdel Test is a simple way to measure how women appear in films. To pass, a film needs at least two named women who talk to each other about something other than a man. It was first described in a 1985 comic strip by Alison Bechdel.

Chart 4 · IMDb rating vs Bechdel rating

IMDb ratings by Bechdel score

The question here is whether audiences rate films differently based on Bechdel score. With 10,000+ points, overplotting is handled with low opacity and small dots. The dashed median lines give a readable summary — and they sit close to each other across all four groups. Bechdel score does not appear to have a strong effect on how audiences rate a film.

A low bar,
but still useful.

The Bechdel Test does not measure the quality of female characters in a film. It only checks their basic presence. A film can fail the test and still have well-written female characters, or pass it and show women in a flat way.

Still, the test is useful for seeing general patterns. Since the 1970s, the share of passing films has slowly grown from around 45% to about 60%. The change is there, but it is gradual.
