And yet.

The Bechdel Test doesn't measure the quality of female representation — it only tracks basic presence. A film can fail the test and be brilliant, or pass it and still be deeply stereotypical.

But as a tool for spotting systemic patterns, it works. From the 1970s to the 2010s, the share of passing films rose from ~45% to ~60%. Progress exists — but it's slow, and largely quantitative rather than qualitative.
